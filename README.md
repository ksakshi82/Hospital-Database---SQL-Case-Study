# Hospital-Database---SQL-Case-Study
A complete MySQL-based analysis of hospital performance, billing, and patient outcomes.


## 📌 Project Overview
Hospitals generate a massive amount of data daily—from appointments and treatments to billing and payments. Without proper analysis, it’s difficult for administration to make informed business decisions. 

This project involves a comprehensive **MySQL-based analysis** of hospital data to understand revenue performance, doctor productivity, patient behavior, and operational efficiency. The primary goal is to convert raw clinical and financial data into meaningful insights that support data-driven decision-making for hospital management.

## ⚠️ Problem Statement
The hospital management needed clarity on several operational and financial blind spots:
* **Revenue Concentration:** Identifying which treatments and doctors drive the most revenue.
* **Cash Flow Bottlenecks:** Tracking outstanding payments that impact financial health.
* **Operational Inefficiencies:** Measuring the direct revenue loss caused by appointment cancellations and no-shows.
* **Resource Allocation:** Uncovering uneven doctor workload distribution and underutilization.

## 🎯 Project Objectives
1. Evaluate hospital revenue and billing performance.
2. Measure doctor workload and department efficiency.
3. Analyze treatment costs and profitability.
4. Assess patient outcomes and repeat visits (retention).
5. Provide actionable business recommendations for hospital management.

## 🗄️ Database Schema & Data Modeling
The database consists of 5 interconnected tables. Foreign Key constraints were implemented to maintain data integrity.

* **Patients** (`patients.csv`): Central demographic and administrative records.
* **Doctors** (`doctors.csv`): Medical staff profiles and operational locations.
* **Appointments** (`appointments.csv`): Scheduling logs connecting patients to doctors.
* **Treatments** (`treatments.csv`): Clinical procedures administered during appointments.
* **Billing** (`billing.csv`): Financial transactions mapping treatment costs to patients.

**Relationship Flow:**
`Patients` & `Doctors` ➔ `Appointments` ➔ `Treatments` ➔ `Billing`

## ❓ Key Business Questions Addressed
Using SQL queries, this project answers the following critical questions:
* What is the total revenue generated by the hospital, and how does it vary month-by-month?
* Which treatments and doctors generate the highest revenue?
* How does revenue distribution vary across doctor specializations?
* What is the distribution of payment statuses (Paid, Pending, Failed)?
* How much revenue is currently outstanding or unpaid?
* Which payment methods are most frequently used by patients?
* How many appointments does each doctor handle, and are there underutilized doctors?
* Which patients visit frequently (retention indicator)?
* How much revenue is contributed by each insurance provider?
* What is the estimated revenue loss due to cancellations and no-shows?

## 💡 Key Insights
1. **Revenue Drivers:** Revenue is heavily driven by a small, concentrated group of treatments and specific doctors.
2. **Workload Imbalance:** Doctor productivity and workload are unevenly distributed across departments.
3. **Financial Leakage:** Outstanding and failed payments are significantly impacting cash flow.
4. **Operational Loss:** Cancellations and no-shows are causing direct, measurable revenue loss.
5. **Insurance Dependency:** There is a heavy revenue dependency on a select few insurance providers.

## 🚀 Business Recommendations
Based on the data analysis, the following strategic actions are recommended:
* **Scale High-Revenue Treatments:** Invest marketing and resources into the most profitable treatments.
* **Optimize Capacity:** Rebalance doctor workloads and implement performance management for underutilized staff.
* **Strengthen Payment Recovery:** Automate follow-ups for 'Pending' and 'Failed' payment statuses.
* **Reduce Cancellations:** Implement an automated SMS/Email reminder system and consider cancellation fees.
* **Diversify Revenue:** Partner with a wider range of insurance providers to reduce financial risk.
